# Music Ducking Under Speech

Background music should make a podcast episode feel produced without making speech harder to understand.

## User Goal

A creator should be able to keep intro, outro, transition, and bed music tasteful while every host and guest remains clear.

## When It Applies

Suggest music ducking only where it protects the finished episode:

- intro music continues under the first spoken line
- outro music starts before the final sentence ends
- segment transition music overlaps the next speaker
- sponsor music bed competes with the read
- chapter bumper runs under a guest response
- saved template music does not fit a new speaker mix

The product should preview the affected moment with the current episode audio, not a generic sample.

## Review States

Use creator-facing labels:

- clear
- review overlap
- speech hard to hear
- music intentionally featured
- needs template update

Each state should explain the likely viewer impact, such as "Guest answer is hard to hear under the bumper."

## Creator Controls

Offer simple actions:

- lower music during speech
- keep music featured
- fade out sooner
- move cue earlier
- move cue later
- apply to matching cues
- save balance to template

Avoid exposing compressor settings, sidechain routing, decibel thresholds, or waveform editing in the default path.

## Template Behavior

Saved ducking choices should carry into reusable show templates only when the creator confirms they fit the show identity. Future episodes should still re-check the balance against new speaker voices, guest volume, sponsor reads, and caption confidence.

Ducking issues that make speech hard to hear should appear in audio and caption review, then block publish readiness only when they affect a spoken moment the viewer needs to understand.
